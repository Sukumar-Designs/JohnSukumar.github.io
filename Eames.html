Problem Statement

Design a children’s toy using cardboard

Focus on creating 3D depth using a 2D material

Sketching Out My Initial Ideas

Brainstorming

I wanted to create a toy stroller as my young cousins always loved to play with their stroller

Coming up with the design was difficult due to the restrictions of cardboard’s 2D nature

Constructing the First Prototype

I was pleased with the structural integrity of my initial prototype, but thought the design was too generic

First Prototype

Refining the Prototype

By using cardbord's ability to curve when partially cut on one side, I made the shape more organic

However, It needed more structural support

Modeling in SolidWorks

I added a bottom support and modeled the stroller in SolidWorks to be sent to the lazer cutter

Cardboard Toy Stroller

Final Stroller

Utilizing cardboard’s ability to curve when partially cut on one side, I made the shape more organic

Cutting into one side of the cardboard, in combination with supports, allowed the stroller to be curved while holding its shape

Placing a toy inside the stroller, allowed me to gain a better sense of how a child may use the toy
